# Paper Reading (ML/AI Research)

> **Objetivo:** Leer y comprender papers de ML/AI en inglés con eficiencia — de abstract a implementación. El inglés académico de ML tiene un vocabulario cerrado de ~500 palabras — una vez que lo dominas, lees cualquier paper.

## Objetivos de aprendizaje

- Leer un paper de ML en 1 hora con comprensión profunda (no solo el abstract)
- Identificar la contribución real de un paper en 5 minutos (abstract + figures + conclusions)
- Extraer la arquitectura/algoritmo central y explicarlo en tus propias palabras
- Escribir un 1-page summary en inglés técnico que otro engineer pueda entender
- Replicar la idea central de un paper en código (sin mirar la implementación oficial)

## Conceptos clave

### The 3-Pass Method (Keshav, Stanford)

```
Pass 1 (5-10 min) — Bird's eye view
  ├─ Read: title, abstract, introduction, conclusions
  ├─ Skim: section headings, figures, tables
  └─ Answer: What's the problem? What's the contribution? Is this paper relevant to me?

Pass 2 (1 hour) — Content absorption
  ├─ Read: entire paper, but skip detailed proofs and implementation minutiae
  ├─ Mark: terms you don't understand, claims that need verification
  ├─ Summarize: main argument in 3-5 bullet points
  └─ Answer: Could I explain this paper to a colleague?

Pass 3 (3-5 hours) — Deep understanding (only for critical papers)
  ├─ Reconstruct: derive the math yourself, re-implement the method
  ├─ Challenge: what assumptions are they making? Are they valid?
  └─ Answer: What would I do differently? What's the next problem?
```

### Academic English for ML — High-Frequency Patterns

```
Contribution framing:
  "We propose [Method], a novel approach to [Problem] that [Key insight]."
  "Unlike prior work, [Method] does not require [Expensive thing]."
  "Our key insight is that [Simple observation] enables [Big result]."

Claim hedging (how researchers avoid overclaiming):
  "Our results suggest that..."    (not "prove")
  "This appears to be..."          (not "is")
  "To the best of our knowledge..."(not "for the first time ever")
  "We hypothesize that..."         (not "we know that")

Comparing to prior work:
  "In contrast to [PriorWork], our method..."
  "[PriorWork] achieved X, but at the cost of Y. We address this by..."
  "While [PriorWork] focused on [Aspect], we consider [Broader aspect]."
```

### Key Vocabulary — 20 words you'll see in every ML paper

| Word | Meaning in context | Example |
|------|-------------------|---------|
| propose | introduce (not "suggest casually") | "We propose a new architecture" |
| leverage | use strategically | "We leverage pre-trained embeddings" |
| mitigate | reduce/address (a problem) | "To mitigate overfitting..." |
| facilitate | enable/make easier | "This facilitates faster convergence" |
| albeit | although (formal) | "Albeit at higher computational cost" |
| akin to | similar to | "Akin to batch normalization" |
| yield | produce (a result) | "This yields a 3% improvement" |
| exhibit | show (a property) | "The model exhibits strong generalization" |
| induce | cause/create | "This induces sparsity in the weights" |
| inherently | by its nature | "The method is inherently parallelizable" |
| constitute | make up/form | "These layers constitute the encoder" |
| derive | obtain mathematically | "We derive a closed-form solution" |
| explicitly | stated directly | "We explicitly model the uncertainty" |
| implicitly | without stating directly | "The model implicitly learns..." |
| substantially | significantly | "Substantially outperforms baselines" |
| albeit | though | "Slower, albeit more accurate" |
| pertaining to | relating to | "Results pertaining to convergence" |
| respectively | in that order | "A and B achieve 90% and 85%, respectively" |
| furthermore | in addition (formal) | "Furthermore, we show..." |
| conversely | in contrast | "Conversely, the smaller model..." |

## Estrategia (2 semanas)

**Semana 1 — Fundamentos + 2 papers**
- Día 1: Ver Stanford CS230 "How to Read Papers" — internalizar 3-pass method
- Día 2-3: Leer "Attention Is All You Need" (Vaswani et al., 2017) — Pass 1 + Pass 2
- Día 4: Escribir 1-page summary de Attention paper en tus propias palabras
- Día 5-6: Leer "LoRA: Low-Rank Adaptation" (Hu et al., 2021) — Pass 1 + Pass 2
- Día 7: Escribir 1-page summary de LoRA — comparar con Attention (¿qué problema resuelve LoRA que Attention no?)

**Semana 2 — Deep dive + producción**
- Día 8-9: Re-leer Attention paper — Pass 3: derivar scaled dot-product attention a mano, entender cada ecuación
- Día 10-11: Re-leer LoRA — Pass 3: implementar LoRA en PyTorch para una capa linear simple
- Día 12: Comparar ambos papers — ¿qué patronos de escritura académica comparten?
- Día 13: Leer un tercer paper de tu elección (ej: "FlashAttention", "DPO", "Llama 3 paper") — Pass 1 autónomo
- Día 14: Escribir mini-review comparando los 3 papers — 2 páginas
